Yuwei Sun

PhD candidate at the University of Tokyo 
Research fellowship at Japan Society for the Promotion of Science
RIKEN Center for Advanced Intelligence Project (AIP) Researcher

My research interests lie in the co-learning mechanism of decentralized and modular neural networks with Minsky's Society of Mind as a guide. My focus is on how knowledge learned by different agents or modalities could be transferred and leveraged to tackle future tasks. Understanding these dynamics would help build generalizable lifelong learning machines.

Biography

I am a PhD student in the Graduate School of Information Science and Technology at the University of Tokyo. I received the B.E. degree in computer science and technology from North China Electric Power University, in 2018, and the M.E. degree (Hons.) in information and communication engineering from the University of Tokyo, in 2021. In 2020, I was the fellow of the Advanced Study Program (ASP) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. I am working as a part-time researcher at the RIKEN Center for Advanced Intelligence Project and a research fellow at the Japan Society for the Promotion of Science. During my PhD, I have spent time at United Nations University and UPenn.

I am a project manager for Microsoft Research Asia D-CORE and JSPS Grants-in-Aid for Scientific Research to develop modular knowledge representation systems for lifelong learning. I am in Moonshot Program by the Cabinet of Japan to bridge artificial and natural intelligence.
