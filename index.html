Yuwei Sun

PhD candidate at the University of Tokyo 
Research fellowship at Japan Society for the Promotion of Science
RIKEN Center for Advanced Intelligence Project (AIP) Researcher

My research interests lie in the interaction and co-learning mechanism of decentralized neural networks. System 1 AI is fast and intuitive while System 2 AI is slow and effortful. I am interested in how knowledge learned by an agent or a specific modality can be transferred and leveraged as experience to tackle future tasks.

Biography

I am a PhD student in the Graduate School of Information Science and Technology at the University of Tokyo. I received the B.E. degree in computer science and technology from North China Electric Power University, in 2018, and the M.E. degree (Hons.) in information and communication engineering from the University of Tokyo, in 2021. In 2020, I was the fellow of the Advanced Study Program (ASP) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. I was a research intern at United Nations University Centre in Federated Learning. I am working as a part-time researcher in the AI Security and Privacy team at the RIKEN Center for Advanced Intelligence Project and a PhD research fellow at the Japan Society for the Promotion of Science. I visited UPenn, Waseda Univ., and Chula Univ..
