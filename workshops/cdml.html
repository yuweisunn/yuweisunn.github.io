Future of Collaborative and Decentralized Machine Learning: Opportunities and Challenges

Date: May 19th

Place: UTokyo (Hongo campus), Engineering Building 2, 4F Room 242

Collaborative and decentralized machine learning (ML) is a rapidly evolving field that presents new opportunities and challenges. In this workshop session, we will explore the opportunities and challenges of collaborative and decentralized ML, with a focus on its future. Our aim is to bring together researchers, practitioners, and policymakers to discuss the latest developments in this field, exchange ideas, and identify key research questions and directions.

17:40-18:10 Panel discussion and Q&A - Ng Chong (United Nations University), Hideya Ochiai, Yuwei Sun (Moderator)

This panel discussion explores the challenges and opportunities associated with the democratization and decentralization of AI. The panelists will address concerns such as misinformation, privacy, and security, emphasizing the need for effective strategies and the role of government policymaking. They will also highlight the potential benefits of a society with distributed agents and their personalization. The panel will emphasize the ongoing need for attention to AI democratization and collaborative efforts to ensure responsible development and deployment.
